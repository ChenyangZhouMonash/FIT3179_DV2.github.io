The visualization of the CO2 emission over the world

The map above is used to show the emissions of CO2 gas all over the world. In the world map above, the depth of the map's color represents the amount of the CO2 emissions, The darker the color, the more CO2 emissions, and vice versa. In order to let us know more detail information about the CO2 of the specified country, the world map would pop a window to show the amount of the CO2 emission of the current country. Also, it could pull the slides to see the CO2 emissions in different years. From the map above, we could find that the regions with a large amount are China, US, Brazil, Russia, Australia and India, and so on.
The chart above is used to show the top 10 countries in GHG emissions from 1990 - 2018. From this chart above, we can know the top 10 countries with highest CO2 emission from 1990 to 2018 are: Brazil, Canada, China, Germany, India, Indonesia, Iran, Japan, Rissia and United States, respectively. Especially, the growth rate of the top 3 countries are obviously increasing since the 1997.
This chart above is mainly used to show the changement of the top 10 countries with the highest CO2 emission from 1990 to 2018. From this chart, it could know the increasing rate of the CO2 emission of the Indonesia is the highest, its changement from the 1.6 to 11.8. The following countries are China and Russia, respectively.
